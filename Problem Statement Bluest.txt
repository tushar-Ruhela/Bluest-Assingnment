Problem Statement

AI-Based Deep Learning Model Monitoring and Drift Detection System

Background

Machine learning models deployed in production are not static—they operate in environments where data and user behavior continuously evolve. Over time, this can lead to a decline in model performance.

Without proper monitoring, these issues often go unnoticed until they significantly impact outcomes.

Objective

Design and develop an AI-based system that can:

Continuously monitor model performance and input data

Detect data drift and concept drift

Trigger alerts or automated retraining mechanisms when needed

Key Requirements

Your solution should:

🧠 Identify both subtle and significant drift patterns

⚡ Provide real-time or near real-time monitoring

🔔 Generate alerts for performance degradation

🔄 Support automated or guided retraining workflows

📊 Provide clear insights and visualizations of model behavior

Problem Context-:

Deployed models face several challenges, including:

🔄 Data drift – changes in input data distribution over time

🧠 Concept drift – changes in the underlying relationships the model has learned

📉 Gradual degradation in prediction accuracy

⚠️ Misalignment between training data and real-world data

Key challenges include:

🔍 Difficulty in detecting drift early

📊 Lack of visibility into model performance in real time

⏱️ Delayed response to performance drops

🔄 Manual and reactive model retraining processes

As a result:

🚫 Models produce inaccurate or unreliable predictions

💸 Business decisions may be affected negatively

📉 Trust in AI systems decreases over time
